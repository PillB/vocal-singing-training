# Vocal training (speaking pedagogy) 
1. Better diction: grab book, rote read same page. For 5 minutes. Overdo mouth movements. Rates of speech: 5,6,7,8 
2. Maintain Volume: Count from 1 to 10 keeping the same energy throughout. Until you naturally run out of breath. 
3. Lift soft pallate: 1-2 min stick tongue out and count to 60. 
4. Improve articulation: count to 60 with pen in mouth focusing on achieving clarity 
5. Neutral ears: prepare a persona: motivador, coach, friend, educator and try it out with a stranger. Begin with a compliment. Also prepare and test a story. Review and improve. Section 4.9.0 how to document stories 
6. How to connect with someone: find out who they are 
7. Record and Review Record a 5-10 minute video of yourself - improv topic; favourite location / food / movie / TV show / video game**Leave 1 full day before review**1) Review Auditory (e.g., Vocal Foundations)2) Review Visual (e.g., movement, hand gestures, body language)3) Transcribe (e.g., highlight all your filler words - or anything that decreases the clarity of your message) Ask chatgpt for most common words we use and grammatical errors 
8. Improve fluency: Metaphors book 
9. 12 week plan: 
 1. Week (1): Pick ONE element to improve (e.g., Volume, Tonality, Facial expression, etc) - work on this for the next 7 days. At the end of the week, **RECORD and REVIEW, and determine if it has improved! If it has NOT improved, then: 
 2. Week (2): Continue to work on Week 1's element for this week. **RECORD and REVIEW again. 
 3. If it HAS improved, then: 
 4. Week (2): Pick ONE new element to improve - and repeat the process. 
 
# Singing training (singing pedagogy) 
1. Vocal fry exercises: breath in for 3 seconds, in a normal speaking voice start doing vocalising an A using vocal fry and good vocal chord closure and good air dosification not being breathy and transition to an A sound hold for maximum amount of time possible. Log. Repeat trying to last slightly longer 1 or 2 seconds more. For 5 minutes. 
2. Using the A sound with good vocal closure (if it helps start with vocal fry to help to find the vocal closure) and dosifying the air and not being breathy in a normal sing like you speak sing the A sound in solfeo so choose 4 or 5 combinations of chord progressions for each repeat 5 times the excercise. 
3. Progress exercise 2 instead of doing the A use 2 stanzas of a song of the songs chosen by user (we chose two songs chosen by the user) sing then in solfeo every couple of words changing the note. with good vocal closure (if it helps start with vocal fry to help to find the vocal closure) and dosifying the air and not being breathy in a normal sing like you speak sing. Repeat 5 times each song. Try to stay in the correct notes. (Try to choose notes appropriate for the song in a couple of the repetitions but also use other chord progressions for variety).
