## A. Vocal Training (Vinh Giang)

### V1 — Better Diction

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Original** | Grab book, rote-read same page 5 min. Overdo mouth movements. Rates of speech: 5,6,7,8 |
| **Enhanced** | Choose a short page. Read the *same* page repeatedly for 5 minutes. Deliberately **over-articulate** (cartoon-clear lips/jaw). Cycle speech rates labeled 5→6→7→8 (comfortable → slightly faster → brisk → challenge), ~1 min each, then free mix. |
| **Why it works** | Exaggeration builds articulatory range; when you “come back to normal,” clarity improves (over-articulation principle used in public-speaking coaching). Rate ladders build control without sacrificing clarity. |
| **Tips** | Soften jaw tension; keep tongue forward enough for consonants; record 30s at start and end to hear improvement. Don’t sacrifice breath for speed. |
| **Common mistakes** | Mumbling at high rates; only moving lips not tongue; changing pages (defeats muscle memory). |
| **Metrics** | Duration practiced (target 5:00); self-clarity 1–5; rate control 1–5; notes. |
| **Audio** | Optional metronome-like pace tones at rates 5–8; recording encouraged. |

### V2 — Maintain Volume

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Original** | Count 1–10 same energy until natural breath out. |
| **Enhanced** | Inhale comfortably. Count 1 to 10 at **one steady energy/volume**—no fade at 8–10, no blast at 1. Repeat until breath runs out naturally. Rest; repeat sets for ~5 min. |
| **Tips** | Think “same distance to the wall,” not louder. Support with gentle abdominal engagement; avoid throat push. |
| **Mistakes** | Trailing off; starting too loud; holding residual air with tension. |
| **Metrics** | Longest complete 1–10 cycles per breath; volume consistency self-score 1–5. |
| **Audio** | Optional steady reference tone level (not pitch target). |

### V3 — Lift Soft Palate

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Original** | 1–2 min tongue out, count to 60. |
| **Enhanced** | Gently stick tongue out (comfortable, not strained). Count to 60 aloud with a **taller oral space** (yawn-feel / “hot potato” soft palate). 1–2 minutes total; stop if jaw/tongue cramp. |
| **Tips** | Imagine fragrance of a rose → soft palate lifts. Keep neck free. |
| **Mistakes** | Forcing tongue too far; nasal collapse; rushing count. |
| **Metrics** | Completed count; resonance openness self-score 1–5; comfort 1–5. |

### V4 — Improve Articulation (Pen)

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Original** | Count to 60 with pen in mouth focusing on clarity. |
| **Enhanced** | Place clean pen/chopstick horizontally between teeth (safe, not biting hard). Count 1–60 aiming for **intelligible** consonants. Then remove pen and count 1–20 noticing ease. |
| **Tips** | Over-work tongue tip and lips; this is resistance training for speech muscles. |
| **Mistakes** | Clenching jaw; unsafe objects; skipping the “pen-off” contrast. |
| **Metrics** | Clarity with pen 1–5; clarity after 1–5; completion. |

### V5 — Neutral Ears (Persona)

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Original** | Prepare persona (motivator, coach, friend, educator); try with stranger; start with compliment; prepare/test story; review/improve. Document stories (section 4.9.0 style). |
| **Enhanced** | (1) Write 4 short persona cards. (2) Craft a 60–90s story with setup–turn–point. (3) Practice alone on video. (4) Optional real interaction: open with a genuine compliment, deliver story, note reaction. (5) Review recording. |
| **Tips** | Neutral ears = don’t self-judge mid-delivery; collect data afterward. Compliments must be specific and true. |
| **Metrics** | Persona readiness 1–5; story structure 1–5; delivery confidence 1–5. |
| **Audio** | Record practice takes. |

### V6 — How to Connect

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Original** | Find out who they are. |
| **Enhanced** | Practice **curiosity loops**: open question → listen → reflect back one detail → deeper question. Role-play 3 scenarios (colleague, acquaintance, new contact). Goal: leave with one real fact about *them*, not a monologue about you. |
| **Tips** | 70/30 listen/speak; match energy; names matter. |
| **Metrics** | Questions asked quality 1–5; listening presence 1–5. |

### V7 — Record & Review

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Original** | 5–10 min video improv (favourite location/food/movie/TV/game). Leave **1 full day** before review. (1) Auditory (2) Visual (3) Transcribe fillers; use tools for common words/grammar. |
| **Enhanced 3-step review** | **Day 0:** Record 5–10 min. **Day +1:** (A) **Auditory** — volume, tonality, pace, diction, breath, filler sounds. (B) **Visual** — posture, face, hands, eye contact with lens. (C) **Transcription** — mark fillers (*um, like, you know*), unclear phrases, grammar. Pick **one** improvement for next week. |
| **Tips** | First review: only note 3 positives + 3 growth points. Avoid all-day self-criticism. |
| **Metrics** | Filler count; clarity 1–5; presence 1–5; completed 3-step checklist. |

### V8 — Improve Fluency (Metaphors)

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Original** | Metaphors book. |
| **Enhanced** | Read/collect metaphors. Practice: take a dry work topic and express it with **one fresh metaphor**. 5 topics × 1 min each. Log best metaphor of the day. |
| **Tips** | Metaphor = concrete image + mapping; avoid cliché stacks. |
| **Metrics** | Metaphors generated count; vividness 1–5. |

### V9 — 12-Week Plan

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Original** | Week 1: pick ONE element, work 7 days, record/review. If not improved → Week 2 continue same. If improved → pick new element. |
| **Enhanced logic** | Elements catalog: Volume, Tonality, Facial expression, Diction, Pace, Filler reduction, Gestures, Story structure, Soft palate/resonance, Connection questions. App tracks current week, element, daily check-ins, end-of-week record/review verdict (`improved` \| `continue`). |
| **Metrics** | Weeks completed; elements mastered list; review scores trend. |

## B. Singing Training (Live Music School)

### S1 — Vocal Fry → Sustained /A/

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Original** | Breath in 3s; speaking voice; vocalise A with vocal fry + good closure + air dosing (not breathy); transition to clean A; hold max; log; try +1–2s; 5 min. |
| **Enhanced** | Inhale 3 counts. Start gentle **vocal fry** on /A/ (creaky door), feel folds contact. Smoothly transition to a **clear speaking-pitch /A/** with steady air (not breathy, not pressed). Hold until natural end. Log seconds. Next rep: aim +1–2s. Stop if strain. |
| **Tips** | Fry is a *finder* for closure, not the end goal. Hydrate. Speak-like pitch first (men: ~A2–D3). |
| **Mistakes** | Pushing fry hard; breathy float; throat squeeze on hold. |
| **Metrics** | Max hold seconds; closure quality 1–5; air dosing 1–5; total practice ≥5 min. |
| **Audio** | Reference A pitch in male mid-low; soft timing ticks for 3s inhale. |

### S2 — /A/ Solfège on Chord Progressions

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Original** | Good closure (fry optional); dosify air; sing A in solfège; 4–5 chord progressions; each ×5. |
| **Enhanced** | Five progressions (men’s mid-low piano). On each chord, sing /A/ on the chord tones (do–mi–sol patterns or root-focused solfège). 5 reps per progression. Prioritize **closure + steady air** over volume. |
| **Progressions** (roots ~C3 range): 1) C–Am–F–G  2) Am–F–C–G  3) G–Em–C–D  4) F–C–Dm–Bb  5) Dm–Bb–F–C |
| **Metrics** | Reps completed /25; pitch comfort 1–5; closure 1–5; breathiness 1–5 (lower better). |
| **Audio** | **Play realistic piano chords** for each progression; loopable. |

### S3 — Song Stanzas Solfège

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Original** | Like S2 but 2 stanzas of user songs (Feel; Better Man — Robbie Williams); solfège every couple of words changing note; good closure; sing-like-you-speak; ×5 each; stay on notes; mix song-appropriate + other progressions. |
| **Enhanced** | User practices two stanzas of *Feel* and *Better Man*. Sing lyrics **solfège-style** (or on /A/ if lyrics distract): change pitch every couple of words with piano progression underneath. 5 reps each song. Some reps in song-friendly key; some in alternate progressions for flexibility. |
| **Tips** | Text first spoken; then spoken on pitch; then sung. Never sacrifice closure for melody. |
| **Metrics** | Reps per song; note accuracy self 1–5; closure 1–5; enjoyment 1–5. |
| **Audio** | Piano progressions in male-friendly keys (e.g., G/C area for Feel-like material). |

## C. Cross-cutting pedagogical principles

1. **Preserve spirit** — homework steps remain the core; we only clarify and scaffold.  
2. **Non-judgmental metrics** — growth language.  
3. **One focus at a time** — mirrors 12-week plan.  
4. **Record to learn** — especially V7 and weekly reviews.  
5. **Air + closure** — unifying singing principle from LMS homework.
